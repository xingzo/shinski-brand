## How Press Release Keywords Actually Work

A press release is **not** a search-traffic play. Nobody searches "DJ Shinski 2 million" or "DJ Shinski YouTube milestone." The keywords in a press release serve three different purposes:

1. **Backlink anchor text** — When music blogs and news sites pick up the story, the words they use (pulled from your release) become the anchor text and surrounding copy that links back to djshinski.com. Google uses that text to understand what your site is about. If those links say "Afrobeat DJ" and "Amapiano mixes," your authority on those terms grows.

2. **Brand entity building** — Google assembles a "knowledge entity" around your name from structured data across the web. A press release on newswires adds signals like "Kenyan DJ," "YouTube creator," "2 million subscribers," and specific genres. This strengthens your Knowledge Panel and how Google describes you in search results.

3. **Journalist language seeding** — Journalists rewrite press releases in their own words, but they borrow your framing. The genre terms and geographic descriptors you use become the language in their articles, which then rank on *their* high-authority domains. You're choosing the vocabulary the internet uses to talk about you.

**Bottom line**: Choose keywords based on *what you want Google and journalists to associate with your brand*, not what people will type to find the press release.
